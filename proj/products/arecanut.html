Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our arecanut products

The main difference lies in the processing method. Red arecanut is boiled with natural colorants before drying, giving it a distinctive red color and stronger flavor profile. White arecanut is simply boiled in clean water and sun-dried, resulting in a milder taste and white appearance. Red arecanut is typically considered more premium and is often used for special occasions and traditional practices, while white arecanut has more versatile applications.

Arecanut should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and moisture. For long-term storage, use airtight containers to prevent humidity absorption, which can lead to mold growth or quality deterioration. Properly stored, dried arecanut can maintain its quality for 1-2 years. Processed products like powders should be kept in sealed containers and used within 6-12 months for optimal quality. Always check for any signs of mold or unusual odor before use.
