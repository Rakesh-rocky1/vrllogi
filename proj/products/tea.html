Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our tea products

Store tea in an airtight container away from light, heat, moisture, and strong odors. A cool, dark pantry is ideal. Avoid storing tea in the refrigerator as the moisture can degrade quality. Our packaging is designed to maintain freshness, but once opened, transfer to an airtight container for best results. Properly stored, most teas maintain quality for 6-12 months.

The main difference lies in processing. Black tea undergoes full oxidation, giving it a robust flavor and darker color, while green tea is minimally oxidized, preserving its natural green color and fresh taste. Black tea typically contains more caffeine and has a stronger flavor profile with malty or fruity notes. Green tea has a lighter, more vegetal flavor and contains higher levels of certain antioxidants like EGCG. Both come from the same plant (Camellia sinensis) but offer different taste experiences and health benefits.

Different teas require different brewing methods for optimal flavor. Black tea: Use freshly boiled water (100째C/212째F) and steep for 3-5 minutes. Green tea: Use water at 80째C/175째F (let boiled water cool for 1-2 minutes) and steep for 2-3 minutes to avoid bitterness. Herbal infusions: Use boiling water and steep for 5-7 minutes to extract full flavor. Masala Chai: Simmer with milk and water for 5-7 minutes to develop spice flavors. For all teas, use about 1 teaspoon of tea per cup (240ml) of water, adjusting to taste.

Our herbal infusions line is fully certified organic. For our traditional teas, we offer both conventional and organic options, clearly labeled on the packaging. Our organic teas are certified by international standards organizations and grown without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers. We work closely with tea gardens that implement sustainable farming practices, and we conduct regular testing to ensure all our products, organic or conventional, are free from harmful residues and meet our strict quality standards.
